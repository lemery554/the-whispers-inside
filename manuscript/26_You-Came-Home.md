# Chapter 26

## You Came Home

The front walk was longer than Brooke remembered.

Her legs shook beneath her. Cooper leaned into the leash instead of pulling, favoring one paw as they crossed the yard. The house remained dark except for the weak yellow light above the front door.

Brooke stepped onto the porch and tried the handle.

Locked.

She knocked softly. "Mom."

Nothing moved inside.

Her phone showed three percent. She called Sarah.

The ringing came through the door, faint but close. Sarah was standing somewhere in the foyer listening to it.

"Mom, open the door."

The call ended.

Brooke knocked harder. Cooper turned toward the street and gave a low growl. A car had stopped at the corner. Its headlights remained on.

"Please."

Sarah's voice came through the door. "Are you alone?"

"Yes."

It was true for the moment.

"Did anyone touch you?"

"No."

"Where were you?"

"Outside."

"With that woman?"

Brooke looked at the dark glass beside the door. She could see only herself and the porch light behind her.

"I met her at the diner."

Silence.

"Who is she?"

"I don't know."

That was also true.

The car at the corner rolled forward. Not fast. Its headlights moved across the neighboring lawns and stopped halfway down the block.

"Mom, let me in."

"You left without telling me."

"I know."

"You took Cooper."

"I know."

"The police have been looking for you all night."

"They found me."

"And you ran."

Brooke pressed her forehead to the door. "Yes."

The dead bolt did not move.

Sarah said, "You told strangers I was dangerous. Then you left our house in the middle of the night to meet someone you do not know."

"I'm sorry."

The apology was too quick. Brooke heard it fail.

Cooper's growl deepened. A woman had stepped out of the car. Brooke could not see her face, only the open coat and hands held away from her sides.

"Brooke?" the woman called. "The officer asked us to keep you where you are."

Us. Another car turned onto the street behind the first.

Brooke struck the door with the side of her fist. "Mom."

"You wanted to leave," Sarah said.

"I was scared."

"Of me."

The woman reached the sidewalk. "Nobody is angry with you."

Cooper barked.

Brooke lowered her voice. "Mom, I should have listened."

The words tasted like surrender.

Inside, a floorboard creaked.

"I shouldn't have left," Brooke continued. "I shouldn't have taken Cooper. I made everything worse."

"Why did you go?"

The woman entered the yard.

Brooke looked over her shoulder. "Stop there."

The woman did. "You are freezing."

"Mom, please."

"Why did you go?" Sarah asked again.

Brooke knew the answer Sarah needed. Not truth exactly. A shape made from true pieces.

"Because I thought you didn't understand me."

The porch stayed silent.

"I thought you were trying to keep me because you didn't trust me. I thought leaving would prove I could take care of myself." Brooke looked at Cooper's lifted paw. "I was wrong."

The second car stopped in front of the house.

Claire's voice came through the door. "Is she there?"

"I'm here," Brooke said.

Something struck the inside of the door. A palm, maybe. Claire's palm placed flat against the wood.

"I didn't take your journal," Claire said.

Brooke closed her eyes. "I know."

She did not know who took it. She knew Claire had told the truth.

"I'm sorry I searched your room."

The apology she had saved and never sent came out sounding smaller than it had on the screen.

Claire began to cry.

Sarah said, "Tell me why you came back."

The woman in the yard took one step closer. Cooper lunged and Brooke nearly lost the leash.

"Because I need you," Brooke said.

The dead bolt clicked once, then stopped.

Brooke felt Sarah waiting behind it.

"Because you're my mother." Her voice broke on the last word without Brooke having to force it. "I love you. Please let me come home."

The dead bolt turned.

The door opened six inches. Sarah stood behind it in Mark's old sweatshirt, her face pale with exhaustion. Claire pressed against her side.

Cooper forced his nose through the opening first.

Sarah looked past Brooke at the people near the sidewalk.

"Come in."

Brooke squeezed through with Cooper and the backpack. Claire reached for her. Brooke let the hug happen because the door was still open and the woman in the yard was moving again.

Sarah shut the door.

The woman knocked immediately. "Mrs. Bennett? Police asked us to remain with her."

Sarah locked the dead bolt. Then the chain. Then she pushed the chair beneath the handle exactly as she had promised.

"She's with her family," Sarah called.

The knocking stopped.

Brooke stood in the foyer with Claire's arms around her and Cooper pressed between their legs. For several seconds relief was stronger than fear. The house smelled like coffee, laundry detergent, and the soup Sarah had made two days earlier. The stairs rose toward Brooke's room. Her charger was upstairs. The window was upstairs.

Sarah touched Brooke's wet hair.

"You're freezing."

"I need to charge my phone."

"Of course."

"And Cooper's hurt."

Sarah crouched to examine his paw. Cooper backed against Brooke, teeth briefly visible.

Sarah stood again.

"He needs rest," she said.

Brooke tried to step toward the stairs.

Claire did not let go.

"Claire."

"I missed you."

"I know."

"You were gone all night."

"Let me go upstairs."

Claire's arms tightened.

Sarah touched Claire's shoulder. "Give her room."

Claire released Brooke immediately.

The obedience frightened Brooke more than the grip.

"Thank you," Brooke said.

She took one step toward the stairs.

Sarah moved between Brooke and the first riser.

Her expression remained warm. Almost relieved.

"Your phone first," Brooke said.

"Mine?"

"I need to call Maya. She helped me."

"You can tell me about her after you sleep."

"I need to tell her I'm safe."

Sarah's face softened further. "You are."

She reached for Brooke's backpack.

Brooke stepped away.

The front-door handle moved once.

The dead bolt held. The chair held.

Sarah did not look toward the door.

She looked only at Brooke.

"You came home," she said.
