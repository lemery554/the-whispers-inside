# Chapter 8: Sit With Me

Claire began following Brooke on Tuesday morning.

At first it was easy to explain. She had been sick. The hospital had separated them. Brooke had promised not to go anywhere and then spent hours behind a set of double doors she could not control.

Claire waited outside the bathroom while Brooke brushed her teeth. She sat on Brooke's bed while Brooke dressed. Downstairs, she moved from chair to counter whenever Brooke crossed the kitchen.

"You're hovering," Brooke said.

"I'm eating."

Claire held a forkful of eggs. Sarah had made them again because Claire asked.

"You can eat from a chair."

"I like it here."

Here meant close enough that their sleeves touched.

Sarah watched them over her coffee. "She had a frightening weekend."

"So did I. I'm not standing on anybody."

Claire smiled. "You can if you want."

Brooke moved to the other side of the island. Claire followed with her plate.

Cooper rose from beneath the table and left the kitchen.

Sarah kept both girls home from school. Claire's pediatrician wanted twenty-four symptom-free hours, and Sarah said Brooke needed sleep. Instead Brooke spent the morning editing while Claire occupied the spare chair beside her desk.

"The clicking is annoying," Claire said.

"Then leave."

"No."

"Compelling strategy."

"Sit with me downstairs."

"I'm working."

"You can work downstairs."

Brooke turned from the monitor. "Why?"

Claire's answer came too quickly. "Because I asked."

She smiled after saying it, trying to make the sentence lighter. Brooke recognized the adjustment. Claire had heard herself sound demanding.

"Give me twenty minutes."

"Ten."

"Thirty now."

"That's not how bargaining works."

Claire watched the screen while Brooke reviewed footage from before the hospital: Claire at lunch leaving the sandwich untouched, Cooper moving behind Brooke's chair, Claire on the stairs reaching for her hand. In the corner of the monitor, the current Claire leaned closer.

"Don't use the part where I throw up."

"I didn't."

"Promise?"

"It's your face. You get a veto."

Claire relaxed. "Okay."

The ordinary response made Brooke feel ashamed of the question forming in her mind.

At noon, Sarah took a work call upstairs. Brooke carried her laptop to the sofa. Claire curled beside her beneath the heavy blanket, shoulder pressed along Brooke's arm. Each time Brooke shifted, Claire shifted too.

The highest-rated comment beneath *My sister got sick* called Claire's behavior a trauma response and told Brooke to be kind. Brooke wanted it to be right.

Claire fell asleep against her. Brooke waited until her breathing deepened, then eased one arm free and stood.

Brooke made it to the kitchen before Claire appeared in the doorway with the blanket around her shoulders.

"You left."

"I got water."

"You could've told me."

Cooper stood in the hall. Claire crouched and held out both hands. "Are you done being weird?"

He turned his head away.

"Cooper."

Brooke touched his collar. The fur beneath it felt warm. "Come on, buddy."

Claire reached for the side of his face.

The growl came without warning.

It was lower than a bark and louder than the sound he had made outside Claire's room. His lips did not lift. His body simply became rigid beneath Brooke's hand.

Claire froze.

Sarah appeared at the top of the stairs with her headset still on. "What happened?"

"He growled at me," Claire said.

Cooper backed behind Brooke.

"Did somebody step on him?" Sarah asked. "Startle him?"

"Claire touched him."

Sarah came down and checked his paws, collar, and ears. Cooper tolerated everything while watching Claire.

"Maybe he's sick," Sarah said.

"He isn't."

"You don't know that."

Claire remained crouched in the hall. Her face had gone blank with hurt.

"It's okay," Brooke told her.

Claire looked at Cooper. "I didn't do anything."

She did not shout at him or reach again. She stood slowly and moved beside Brooke instead, close enough that their hands brushed.

Sarah said they would call the veterinarian. Brooke nodded. Maybe there was a sore place beneath his fur, or some smell from the hospital that had not washed away.

That night, Claire appeared in Brooke's doorway carrying her pillow.

"No."

"I didn't ask yet."

"You brought a pillow."

"Can I sleep on the floor?"

"You have a bed."

"I don't want to be alone."

Brooke looked past her toward the dark hall. Cooper lay at the foot of Brooke's bed, head raised.

"I'll sit with you until you fall asleep," Brooke said.

Claire considered this. "In my room?"

"That is where your bed lives."

Brooke followed her across the hall. Claire climbed beneath the covers and took Brooke's wrist, holding it lightly against the mattress.

"You don't have to hold on."

"Do what?"

"Hold me here."

Claire loosened her fingers. "I'm just holding your hand."

Brooke stayed until Claire's eyes closed, then slipped her hand free.
