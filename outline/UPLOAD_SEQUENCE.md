Locks Brooke's public video thread across the book: what she posts, when, how the audience grows, what the comments do, the survival value each upload carries, and what Anna learns from it. The channel is **unbranded, under Brooke's own name** (TWI-006), so titles are plain and honest, not clickbait — consistent with a girl who records to preserve truth, not to perform.

The uploads are the mechanism by which "understood, not chosen" (STORY_RULES Rule 18) actually operates.

**Anna's viewing window:** Anna first meets Brooke's uploads through the public algorithm around **Day 7** and begins working the archive **Day 8** (Ch 8–9). So for Uploads 1–2 (posted Days 3 and 6), "what Anna learns" is retroactive — she binges the back-catalogue once she finds the channel.

## View-growth curve

Gradual, then a single clear jump. Uploads 1–3 stay marginal (mockery, dismissal) — this matters, because the world *not* believing Brooke is part of her isolation. Upload 4 is the inflection (matching reports arrive); Upload 5 (Day 14) is the **jump** to a real audience; Uploads 6 keeps a large but institutionally-drowned audience (warnings get buried). The curve mirrors the institutional phases: the audience grows just as the platforms begin steering everyone toward "togetherness," so Brooke's reach and her irrelevance rise together.

## Survival-value spine (what the network actually learns)

Dogs detect the changed → the recovered "remember everything" and use it → the changed cannot bear isolation and escalate toward it → institutions are steering people home. Upload 5 carries the densest cluster; Uploads 4 and 6 frame it.
