# Final Days Continuity Ledger

**Scope:** Chapters 23 through 29
**Status:** Working continuity lock for the developmental rewrite

## Day convention

Story days change at midnight. Chapters 23 through 25 begin late on Day 23 and continue through the overnight pursuit into the pre-dawn hours of Day 24. Chapter 26 occurs before dawn on Day 25 only if an intervening calendar day is explicitly dramatized. The current manuscript does not contain that intervening day.

To remove the contradiction, the developmental rewrite should use this continuous sequence:

- **Day 23, evening:** Chapter 23 preparation and Chapter 24 diner meeting
- **Day 23 into Day 24:** Chapter 25 pursuit
- **Day 24, before dawn:** Chapter 26 front-door bargain
- **Day 24, dawn:** Chapter 27 siege and escape
- **Day 24, early morning:** Chapters 28 and 29 drive and reveal

If the author later wants the climax on Day 26, the preceding timeline must add two fully accounted days. Do not change dates silently.

## Chapter 23: Pack Light

### Brooke

- At home, physically rested enough to plan but emotionally depleted.
- Packs camera, external drive, phone charger, cash, letter photographs, flashlight, and practical clothing.
- Deletes or hides route footage that could expose the window plan.
- Leaves no public upload revealing the escape route.

### Cooper

- Physically sound at the start.
- Chooses Brooke's side of the closed door.

### Devices and files

- Phone charged enough for the diner trip.
- Camera battery and external drive packed.
- Unsent message to Mark remains local.

## Chapter 24: Outside

### Time and weather

- Late evening.
- Cold enough for cardigan, coat, and later dangerous exposure.
- Roads and roof should become wet only if rain, mist, or frost is established here or in Chapter 25.

### Brooke

- Leaves with backpack and Cooper.
- Starts local phone audio before entering the diner.

### Anna

- At diner before Brooke arrives.
- Coffee spill creates the stain on the blue cuff.
- Does not touch Brooke or Cooper.
- Pays inside after Brooke leaves.

### Files

## Chapter 25: Contact

### Route

- Diner to Birch, Weller, gas station, alley, back road, wooded strip, Bennett development.
- Pursuers act independently through the missing-minor bulletin and ordinary calls.

### Brooke

- Records portions of the pursuit.
- Leaves backpack at the gas-station outlet, then recovers it after it is returned.
- Becomes cold, wet, exhausted, and increasingly unable to protect Cooper.
- Turns home because public shelter is compromised, battery is failing, Cooper is injured, and Sarah may open the door.

### Cooper

- Scrapes or strains one paw during the pursuit.
- Limp becomes clear before Brooke reaches home.
- Injury remains painful but non-catastrophic through the ending.

### Devices and files

- Diner audio remains saved locally.
- Gas-station/pursuit video remains local.
- Phone battery falls to approximately three percent at the front door.
- Camera, drive, cash, and photographs remain in backpack.

### Anna

- Leaves the diner after paying.
- Does not join the visible pursuit.
- Moves her car to Hawthorne, two streets from the Bennett house, using public address information and prior surveillance knowledge.
- Waits because Brooke's videos, journal, and repeated public pursuit demonstrate that approaching would make Brooke run.

## Chapter 26: You Came Home

### Time

- Pre-dawn, continuous with Chapter 25.

### Brooke

- Wet, cold, exhausted, and carrying the backpack.
- Phone at approximately three percent.
- Consciously chooses the known house over the people closing around her.

### Cooper

- Favors the injured paw.
- Remains between Brooke and others when possible.

### Sarah and Claire

- Sarah knows about the diner woman from the missing-minor response.
- Sarah delays opening because unknown people are arriving with Brooke and she wants Brooke's voluntary return confirmed.
- Claire directly denies taking the journal.
- Brooke apologizes for searching Claire's room.

### Objects

- Front door is locked, chained after entry, and braced with a chair.
- Backpack remains with Brooke until the upstairs negotiation.

## Chapter 27: Separation Distress

### Time and light

- Dawn begins during the siege.
- Brooke's rear window and roof remain dim enough for concealment but not completely black.

### Brooke

- Reaches bedroom with backpack and Cooper.
- Barricades inward-opening door with chair and low dresser.
- Connects phone to charger.
- Records a local warning on camera.
- Calls 911 from the partially charged phone.
- Packs phone, camera, and external drive back into backpack before escape.

### 911

- Address identifies the active missing-minor return.
- Dispatcher uses Brooke's name from the record.
- Call is coded as welfare return while shotgun remains secured.
- Reduced staffing delays response.
- No unit arrives before escape.

### Firearm

- Electronic safe in attached garage.
- Sarah knows combination from Mark's prior emergency instruction.
- Twelve-gauge pump shotgun.
- 2¾-inch low-recoil No. 4 buckshot.
- Sarah orders Claire downstairs.
- Sarah hears the open window and Cooper on the sill.
- Brooke is outside and below the window line.
- Sarah fires once, low and diagonally through latch-side jamb.
- Shot damages latch, dresser corner, floor, and side wall.
- Intent is barrier breach, not aiming at Brooke, though the act carries lethal risk.

### Escape

- Backpack goes onto roof first.
- Cooper is guided through by harness.
- Brooke and Cooper cross wet or slick shingles only if moisture was established earlier.
- Cooper lands badly and aggravates the paw.
- Neighboring car alarm and porch lights react to blast.

### Files

- Siege recording remains on camera or camera card.
- 911 call exists in system records.
- Nothing uploads during escape.

## Chapter 28: Headlights

### Anna's timing

- Parked on Hawthorne, two streets away.
- Hears gunshot.
- Moves to the likely escape street after the shot, then turns on headlights when Brooke enters view.
- Does not approach house or participate in pursuit.

### Brooke

- Sees Sarah with shotgun and consciously chooses the car.
- Closes passenger door herself.
- Receives Sarah's text and leaves it unanswered.
- Directly asks whether Anna took the journal.
- Starts a new local audio recording during the drive.
- Weighs Anna's suspicious knowledge against Sarah's immediate violence and institutional return policy.

### Cooper

- Enters back seat.
- Receives water and basic paw cleaning.
- Tolerates Anna because she remains still and non-advancing.
- Does not sleep fully.

### Anna

- Has ordinary water, towel, gauze, and antiseptic in car.
- Names a dog-friendly motel beyond the county line as the first destination.
- Pulls into a scenic turnout and parks before Brooke initiates the hug.
- Does not reach first and releases before Brooke does.

### Sarah

- Turns toward garage after Anna drives away, implying a possible attempt to follow.
- This remains an intentional unresolved threat unless the ending-position decision later requires clarification.

## Chapter 29: Continue Learning

### Time

- Early morning, same continuous drive.

### Brooke

- Sleeps against passenger window.
- Phone remains facedown with final-drive recording running.
- Sarah's message remains unanswered.
- Camera roll contains the beach image, preserving her connection to Claire.

### Cooper

- Awake in back seat.
- Recognizes or reacts to the familiar stolen journal and Anna's behavior.
- Gives an uncertain whine rather than a full alarm.

### Anna

- Drives a long empty stretch with one hand while opening the console.
- Retrieves the stolen journal from beneath a folded map.
- Reads the beach passage and the camera/trust entry.
- No final green-eye effect.

### Ending proof

- Journal possession proves entry, theft, and study.
- Folded page confirms repeated reading.
- Final line remains: `Anna turned the page and continued learning how to love her.`
