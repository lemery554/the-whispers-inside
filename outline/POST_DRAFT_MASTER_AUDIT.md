# Post-Draft Master Audit

**Status:** First-draft diagnostic, completed July 16, 2026
**Scope:** Prologue and Chapters 1 through 29
**Purpose:** Identify the work required before deciding the final degree of ending closure.

This audit does not treat the current draft as publication-ready. It identifies what the first developmental revision must accomplish while protecting the premise, mystery, and ending.

## Executive assessment

The manuscript has a complete and coherent psychological-horror architecture. Its strongest material is the family progression from ordinary affection to possessive love, Brooke's use of recorded evidence, the institutional shift toward reunion, and Anna's learned patience. The final sequence pays off the central idea: Brooke escapes visible coercion by choosing a form of coercion designed to feel voluntary.

The current draft is approximately **45,700 words**, excluding the manuscript README. The project target remains **85,000 to 100,000 words**. This is not a small line-editing deficit. The draft currently functions as a compressed complete novel or a detailed narrative draft. The next pass must develop scenes, relationships, transitions, setting, and consequences. It must not double the length through repetition or decorative description.

The ending-position decision should remain deferred until the expanded manuscript has been read as a whole.

## Gate order

1. Resolve critical continuity and credibility defects.
2. Build a scene-development plan that earns the target length.
3. Strengthen character arcs and emotional payoffs.
4. Expand institutional progression and found media.
5. Rebalance pacing and horror escalation.
6. Re-run clue fairness and Anna-knowledge audits.
7. Perform a commercial-prose and repetition pass.
8. Decide whether the ending is fully standalone or sequel-capable.

## 1. Continuity and canon audit

### Findings already resolved in Batch 7

- Sarah learns about the diner meeting through the missing-minor response rather than unexplained knowledge.
- The people outside the Bennett house repeat an instruction traceable to the officer who lost Brooke.
- Sarah initially keeps the door closed because she fears Brooke has brought unknown people to the household and wants Brooke's voluntary return confirmed.
- The 911 system identifies Brooke through the active missing-minor return.
- The call is downgraded while the shotgun remains secured, and reduced staffing delays response.
- Sarah knows the electronic safe combination because Mark required her to learn it for emergencies.
- The shotgun, ammunition, intended breach point, Claire's location, Brooke's position, damage path, and neighborhood response are now specified.
- Anna remained near the house after the diner, parked two streets away, and heard the shot.
- Brooke remains suspicious of Anna, directly asks about the journal, and records the drive.
- The final green-eye marker has been removed.

### Remaining continuity risks

#### C1. Day labels and light conditions — resolved in planning

The climax was labeled Day 25 at the door and Day 26 in the bedroom, but the prose moves continuously from the Day 23 diner through an overnight pursuit into a colorless morning.

**Resolution:** Story days change at midnight. The diner occurs Day 23, and Chapters 25 through 29 continue across midnight into the Day 24 ending. The chapter outline, timeline, scene inventory, and supporting plans have been corrected.

#### C2. Anna's post-diner surveillance duration

Chapter 28 now establishes Hawthorne as her waiting location, but the timeline should specify when she leaves the diner area, when she parks, and how she avoids being part of the public pursuit.

**Required repair:** Add the surveillance route to the timeline and Anna knowledge matrix without giving Brooke new certainty.

#### C3. Brooke's recording remains unresolved

Brooke records the diner, pursuit, siege, and final drive, but the draft does not establish whether those files remain local, upload automatically, or become inaccessible.

**Required repair:** Track each recording, device, battery level, storage location, and upload state through the final chapter.

#### C4. Sarah's likely pursuit

Chapter 28 shows Sarah turning toward the garage, which suggests she may attempt to follow. The manuscript ends before confirming whether she does.

**Required repair:** Decide whether this is a deliberate unresolved threat or an accidental action cue. The ending does not need to show a chase, but the implication must be intentional.

#### C5. Weather and clothing continuity

The final overnight pursuit, wet roof, cold clothes, bare feet, cardigan, dog injury, and dawn drive need a chapter-by-chapter continuity sheet.

**Required repair:** Add a compact physical-state ledger for Days 23 through 26.

## 2. Character-arc and emotional-payoff review

### Brooke

Her arc is conceptually strong: she begins believing recorded distance protects truth, becomes dependent on evidence when reassurance fails, exposes herself while documenting others, and finally trusts a person who learned her through those records.

The draft needs more lived experience between these turns. Brooke's filmmaking ambition, school identity, loneliness, adoption wound, and guilt over Claire are often invoked at plot pressure points but are not yet developed across enough ordinary scenes.

**Revision need:** Add scenes in which Brooke makes choices unrelated to immediate danger, fails socially, edits creatively, and experiences the cost and appeal of being known through a camera.

### Claire

Claire is lovable and specific in the opening chapters, and the beach memory is the strongest emotional thread in the draft. Her later progression is effective, but the compressed middle risks turning her into a repeated proximity mechanism.

**Revision need:** Add varied functional behavior after recovery, including schoolwork, food, humor, boredom, and attempts to behave normally. Preserve brief Claire fragments without turning them into recoveries or warnings.

### Sarah

Sarah's pre-illness conflict with Brooke and post-change control form a strong tragic line. Her exhaustion while Mark is absent needs more page time before her purge so the reader feels the loss of the competent, imperfect mother rather than mainly the arrival of the controlling one.

**Revision need:** Expand Sarah's practical burden, her hurt about Brooke's biological mother, and moments when her restrictions are understandable before they become intolerable.

### Mark

The unknown fate is valid and should remain mysterious. The current draft needs his absence to exert more continuous emotional pressure.

**Revision need:** Strengthen the progression of texts, calls, voicemail fragments, practical promises, and final silence. Do not solve his fate.

### Anna

Her knowledge sources and behavior are fair. The risk is over-perfection. The coffee spill and frustrated correction help, but the full manuscript should contain enough minor imperfections that her patience reads as human before it reads as engineered.

**Revision need:** Preserve her narrow truthfulness while adding hesitation, mundane limitations, and moments Brooke can interpret in more than one way.

### Cooper

Cooper works as warning system, companion, and final unease. Avoid making every reaction perfectly diagnostic.

**Revision need:** Include ordinary dog behavior and occasional ambiguous reactions so his meaningful warnings retain power.

## 3. Pacing and chapter-length assessment

### Current measurements

- Total draft: approximately **45,700 words**
- Target: **85,000 to 100,000 words**
- Average unit length: approximately **1,524 words** across the prologue and 29 chapters
- Shortest chapter: Chapter 29, approximately **500 words**
- Many later chapters fall between **800 and 1,700 words**

The chapter structure is viable, but most units are below the project's intended 2,000 to 3,500-word range.

### Expansion priorities

Add length through:

- ordinary family life before and between escalations
- school and town scenes that demonstrate social change
- Brooke's filmmaking process and upload consequences
- hospital procedure and physical experience
- Mark's communications
- aftermath and decision-making inside scenes
- setting, travel, and physical logistics
- dialogue that reveals competing interpretations
- institutional progression that Brooke directly encounters

Do not add length through:

- repeated hugging or door-scratching scenes
- repeated observation lists containing the same rules
- additional scientific explanations
- new mythology
- extra named supporting characters without a structural purpose
- prolonged chase action
- repeated green-eye sightings

### Tension curve

The broad escalation is sound:

- Prologue through Chapter 3: baseline and public unease
- Chapters 4 through 9: purge and deniable change
- Chapters 10 through 17: recognition and family spread
- Chapters 18 through 23: institutional capture and house intrusion
- Chapters 24 through 29: flight, return, siege, false rescue, reveal

The thinnest stretch is Chapters 7 through 9, where major discoveries arrive in very short chapters. Chapters 18 through 23 also need more lived consequence between institutional developments.

## 4. Mystery and clue-fairness audit

The Anna clue chain remains fundamentally fair:

1. Keypad code appears in public family footage.
2. Brooke discovers and privatizes it too late.
3. Cooper and shifted objects suggest entry.
4. Adoption materials are disturbed.
5. The journal disappears.
6. Anna knows the biological mother's name and letter language.
7. Anna's styling echoes photographs.
8. Anna uses Brooke's own language and respects the boundaries Brooke publicly and privately describes.
9. Anna waits near the house because direct pursuit has repeatedly failed.
10. The journal in the final scene confirms study rather than kinship.

### Risks

- Brooke may appear too slow if the expanded draft repeatedly emphasizes the journal suspicion without adding stronger immediate reasons to choose Anna.
- Anna's source trail can become too obvious if every clue is restated.
- The final chapter should confirm the pattern, not explain every inference.

**Required repair:** During revision, track what Brooke suspects at the end of every Anna-related scene. Her belief should move gradually from coincidence, to hope, to suspicion, to desperate conditional trust.

## 5. Found-media balance review

Found media is distributed across the book, but most inserts are currently brief. The manuscript likely falls below the desired **10 to 15 percent** by word share even though many chapters contain a message, clip, alert, or comment.

### Best expansion opportunities

- Brooke's early low-view uploads and mocking comments
- hospital notices and discharge language
- Mark's texts and broken calls
- school counselor messages
- news wording changes across several broadcasts
- survivor comments that contradict official reassurance
- moderation, search ranking, and missing-minor alerts
- 911 transcript fragments where prose benefits from exact wording

Found media must remain material Brooke creates, receives, sees, or reviews. It should never become an omniscient world-information shortcut.

## 6. Commercial-prose and repetition audit

Automated paragraph review found approximately **1,100 narrative paragraphs of five words or fewer**, about **35 percent** of the draft's narrative paragraphs. Dialogue naturally creates short paragraphs, so this is not a direct defect count. It does confirm that the draft still carries a compressed manuscript rhythm in many chapters.

### Revision priorities

- Combine adjacent action and interpretation when they belong to the same beat.
- Preserve standard paragraph breaks for speaker changes.
- Reserve isolated narration for genuine turns.
- Let setting, body language, and conflicting thought coexist in fuller paragraphs.
- Reduce repeated constructions such as immediate interpretation after dialogue, repeated silence beats, and repeated descriptions of answers being technically true.
- Maintain the ban on em dashes in project guidance and final prose.

### Repetition families to track

- "That was true."
- "Not an answer."
- "She waited."
- "The words..."
- "For a moment..."
- characters becoming still or going quiet before a reveal
- Cooper growling as the primary transition into danger
- Brooke looking at the camera or screen to authorize an interpretation

These motifs are useful. They need deliberate spacing so they remain motifs rather than default sentence solutions.

## 7. Structural-gap assessment

The manuscript does not need more plot. It needs more novel.

The first developmental rewrite should retain the 29-chapter architecture unless expansion proves a chapter is carrying two complete dramatic movements. The most likely productive additions are scene extensions and a small number of chapter splits, not new subplots.

### High-value development zones

1. Chapters 1 through 3: deepen ordinary family life, filmmaking, and Brooke's social position.
2. Chapters 6 through 9: expand hospital aftermath and the false relief of recovery.
3. Chapters 11 through 15: make school and public behavior feel lived rather than summarized.
4. Chapters 16 through 21: develop Sarah's burden, purge, recovery, institutional messaging, and Anna's invisible intrusion traces.
5. Chapters 22 through 25: strengthen escape preparation, online consequences, and Brooke's reasons for trusting the diner meeting.
6. Chapters 26 through 29: preserve speed but add physical and emotional aftermath only where it increases credibility.

## 8. Ending decision gate

Do not decide standalone versus sequel-capable solely from the current compressed ending.

The decision becomes ready when:

- the manuscript reaches a credible full-novel length through earned development
- Brooke, Claire, Sarah, and Mark have received their intended emotional page weight
- the institutional arc is visible without explanation
- the final third no longer feels rushed relative to the opening
- a skeptical-reader pass confirms that Anna's reveal is both fair and emotionally complete

Mark's fate may remain unknown in any option. Mystery is not the same as missing closure.
