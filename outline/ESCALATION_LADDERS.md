# Claire & Sarah Escalation Ladders — TWI-105

Two failure ladders that run parallel to Anna's success ladder (`outline/ANNA_TRUST_LADDER.md`, TWI-104). Where Anna demonstrates the whispers' *later* learned-psychology stage, Claire and Sarah demonstrate the two earlier stages that fail — and their failures are what the collective learns from. Built from locked canon in `bible/infection/EVOLUTION.md`, `bible/infection/RULES.md` (the 10-step isolation escalation), `bible/characters/MAIN_CAST.md`, and `outline/CHARACTER_ARCS.md`. Day/chapter placements trace `outline/CHAPTER_OUTLINE.md` (TWI-103).

Design constraints (all locked):
- **Distinct methods.** Claire escalates through *proximity and force*; Sarah through *emotion, authority, and guilt*. They must never blur into one interchangeable "clingy relative" behavior.
- **Gradual.** Each rung is a small, deniable step past the last, so Brooke (and the reader) can rationalize it until she can't.
- **Emotionally real.** Every rung is sincere from the inside — neither is performing malice (`EVOLUTION.md` "Emotional truth").
- **Synchronized with whisper evolution.** Claire tracks the *early stage* (proximity commands); Sarah tracks the *middle stage* (emotional leverage). Their failures teach the collective what Anna later executes.
- **Canon guardrails.** Both retain memory, cannot state a direct falsehood (`RULES.md`), feel pain but don't protect against it, and surface brief personality fragments only under the locked triggers.

The 10-step isolation escalation from `RULES.md` is the shared spine both ladders climb: **1 Asking · 2 Calling · 3 Pleading · 4 Crying · 5 Bargaining · 6 Repeating private memories · 7 Guilt · 8 Scratching · 9 Breaking barriers · 10 Using tools or weapons.** Each rung below notes which step it occupies. The horror is that the *same ten steps* read completely differently through a 13-year-old sister's body and a mother's.

## Claire — the Proximity Ladder (early-stage whispers: force obtains contact but destroys trust)

Claire is the collective's first, worst-calibrated attempt. She wants closeness and simply takes it, with no model of how taking it makes Brooke flee. She never understands she has changed, which is why she never modulates. Her ladder is almost entirely physical and it climbs fast.

### C1 — Ordinary clinginess *(pre-illness baseline)*
- **Step:** 1 (Asking).
- **Day/Chapter:** Days 1–3 / Ch 1–3.
- **Behavior:** Borrows Brooke's clothes without asking, wants in on jokes and trips, needles Brooke about Sarah. Established as *normal Claire* so the first post-purge rung is deniable.
- **Fragment/tell:** None — this is the real Claire, and the baseline the reader will later measure loss against.
- **Why it matters:** The escalation must start indistinguishable from who she already was.

### C2 — The reaching hand *(purge onset)*
- **Step:** 1→2 (Asking → Calling).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 4 / Ch 4–5.
- **Behavior:** As the purge begins, Claire reaches for Brooke's hand. Read as a frightened, sick child wanting comfort.
- **Fragment/tell:** Cooper refuses to settle near her *before* visible symptoms (animal detection, `RULES.md`).
- **Deniability:** Total — any sick 13-year-old reaches for her sister.

### C3 — "Sit with me" *(constant proximity)*
- **Step:** 2→3 (Calling → Pleading).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 7 / Ch 8.
- **Behavior:** Post-recovery, wants Brooke physically present at all times; follows her room to room; asks Brooke to stay while she falls asleep. She does not yet maintain an unconscious restraining grip after sleep.
- **Fragment/tell:** Cooper growls at Claire for the first time. Claire does **not** retaliate — the restraint fragment is already load-bearing.
- **Deniability:** High — reads as post-illness trauma/attachment, which Sarah explicitly argues.

### C4 — Watching, not resting *(proximity without need)*
- **Step:** 3 (Pleading, nonverbal).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 8 / Ch 9, escalating to Day 15 / Ch 16.
- **Behavior:** Claire watches Brooke sleep instead of sleeping herself; later watches Brooke rather than staying beside sick Sarah. The muted-hunger/dulled-self-preservation wrongness (`RULES.md`) shows here — she doesn't rest because rest is self-directed and self-direction is fading.
- **Fragment/tell:** Near-constant humming (her nervous tic, now dominant per MAIN_CAST); finishing Brooke's sentences.
- **Deniability:** Thinning — Brooke starts her observation list.

### C5 — Private memory as a lure *(memory weaponized)*
- **Step:** 6 (Repeating private memories) — Claire skips bargaining, because early-stage whispers don't yet understand negotiation.
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 9 / Ch 10 ("What They Remember").
- **Behavior:** Uses a shared private memory to coax Brooke closer.
- **Fragment/tell:** Claire loosens a painful grip for exactly one second (trigger: interruption/self-recognition). Lasts seconds, not minutes; does not recur identically (`RULES.md`).
- **Deniability:** Low — Brooke logs "They remember everything."

### C6 — Breaking the lock *(first undeniable violence)*
- **Step:** 9 (Breaking barriers) — Claire escalates from the memory-lure straight to force, skipping the middle rungs. This is the ladder's defining feature: **early-stage whispers reach for force the moment gentler approaches fail**, which is exactly why Claire fails.
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 12 / Ch 13 ("There You Are"). The memory-lure (C5, Day 9) now precedes this breakthrough, so Claire's rungs ascend in story-day order.
- **Behavior:** Brooke refuses to open the bathroom door; Claire breaks the lock and enters **without anger**. She only wants a hug.
- **Fragment/tell:** Injures her hand on the lock and does not react in the moment (pain felt, self-preservation absent, `RULES.md` "Strength and pain"). A private wince or later tending may show the pain registered.
- **Deniability:** Gone. This is the rung where Brooke stops accepting "trauma" as a complete explanation. **This is the failure the collective learns from** — force got contact and destroyed trust (`EVOLUTION.md` early stage).

### C7 — The crack: crying without knowing why *(reverse rung)*
- **Step:** 4 (Crying) — but inverted: not a tactic, a genuine fragment surfacing.
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 13 / Ch 14 ("Sand").
- **Behavior:** During a possessive episode Claire cries without knowing why and briefly loosens her hold. Triggered by the deeply anchored beach memory + the echo of Brooke's cruel pre-illness line (EM-01; the locked emotional-memory trigger).
- **Fragment/tell:** This *is* the fragment — the clearest proof the real Claire persists.
- **Function:** The one rung that climbs *down* the ladder for a moment, which makes the loss legible and escape morally harder.

### C8 — Restraint *(strategy replaces force)*
- **Step:** between 9 and 10 — restraint replacing force.
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 20 / Ch 21.
- **Behavior:** Claire stays in her room after Brooke asks for distance. When Brooke hears an indistinct murmur, Claire truthfully says she was telling herself Brooke needs space.
- **Fragment/tell:** The self-talk is deniable. Retained memory, ordinary observation, and private whisper guidance all remain possible.
- **Function:** Shows strategy shifting from Claire's failed force toward Anna's patience without proving a collective exchange.

### C9 — Present, restrained, at the end *(the fragment that never breaks)*
- **Step:** N/A — the ladder's terminal proof is what Claire *won't* do.
- **Day/Chapter:** Climax / final sequence.
- **Behavior:** Claire is part of the changed collective (e.g., outside the barricade, Ch 27) but **never harms Cooper** — the surviving restraint fragment holds to the end (MAIN_CAST; the protective-threat trigger).
- **Function:** Right up to the last, one thread of the real Claire persists. Her ending state (changed, memory intact, "recovered" by every visible metric) is accepted in-world as a good outcome — the horror.

**Claire's ladder in one line:** asks → takes → breaks → learns to stop. Force works and ruins everything, which is the lesson Anna later embodies.

## Sarah — the Devotion Ladder (middle-stage whispers: love and guilt fail when control becomes visible)

Sarah is the collective's second, more sophisticated attempt. She never breaks a lock to get to Brooke; she uses concern, memory, family role, and guilt — genuine maternal love turned into an instrument. Her ladder is almost entirely *emotional* until the very top, where it terminates in the gun. Her wound (the workshop) is pre-loaded before she ever changes, so every later "protective" line carries a second meaning.

### S1 — Protection as love *(pre-illness wound)*
- **Step:** 1 (Asking) / 7 (Guilt) in embryo.
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 2 / Ch 2 ("Permission").
- **Behavior:** Secretly cancels Brooke's film workshop because travel feels unsafe with Mark away. Brooke discovers it herself; the fight ends unresolved with "You don't get to keep me forever just because she didn't."
- **Tell:** The word *keep*. Planted here, detonated after she changes.
- **Function:** Establishes that Sarah's love and her control are already inseparable **before** the illness — so the change doesn't invent her behavior, it removes the brakes.

### S2 — "Having you home" *(changed warmth)*
- **Step:** 1 (Asking).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 16 / Ch 17.
- **Behavior:** Post-purge, believable maternal warmth. Checks Brooke's breathing while she sleeps. Frames vigilance as love.
- **Technically true statement:** Reassurances about safety that are literally true but reweighted toward control (`RULES.md` direct-lying rule; MAIN_CAST "I'm just keeping you safe").
- **Deniability:** Total — this is just a relieved mother.

### S3 — Restriction as care *(movement narrows)*
- **Step:** 1→2 (Asking → Calling).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 17 / Ch 18.
- **Behavior:** Restricts Brooke's movement, "for now," while news and school validate keeping recovered families together. Household control aligns with institutional drift.
- **Tell:** Sarah's language and the institutions' reunion language converge (IN-adjacent; different systems, same words).
- **Deniability:** High — everyone official agrees with her.

### S4 — The old fight, reused *(guilt + memory)*
- **Step:** 6→7 (Repeating private memories → Guilt).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 20 / Ch 21.
- **Behavior:** Uses love and the *unresolved workshop fight itself* to keep Brooke home — the guilt Brooke already carries becomes the lever. "After everything, you still want to leave me."
- **Tell:** The double meaning of "keep you" is now fully active.
- **Deniability:** Low — Brooke recognizes the manipulation but still feels the guilt, which is the point. **This is Sarah's core method: precise, sincere guilt** (`EVOLUTION.md` middle stage).

### S5 — The bargain at the door *(conditional love)*
- **Step:** 5 (Bargaining) + 3–4 (Pleading/Crying).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 25 / Ch 26 ("You Came Home").
- **Behavior:** Refuses to let Brooke back inside (with strangers pursuing) until Brooke apologizes and emotionally reaches her. Opens from genuine maternal attachment, locks the strangers out — then turns her attention fully onto Brooke.
- **Technically true statement:** Cannot directly lie about safety, so she answers *around* the question of whether Brooke is safe with her (`RULES.md`).
- **Tell:** Ends on "You came home." Brooke survives by using love language she doesn't mean — becoming briefly like what she fears.
- **Deniability:** Gone. The care is now explicitly a cage.

### S6 — The gun through the door *(control becomes force)*
- **Step:** 8→9→10 (Scratching → Breaking barriers → Using tools or weapons).
- **Day/Chapter:** Day 26 / Ch 27 ("Separation Distress").
- **Behavior:** Bedroom siege. Sarah and Claire escalate outside the barricade; Sarah remembers the location of Mark's secured firearm and **fires through Brooke's bedroom door.** The terminal expression of protection turned into violence against the person she's protecting.
- **Tell:** She injures nothing of her own concern doing it; the violence is sincere, not malicious in her own understanding.
- **Function:** **This is Sarah's failure the collective learns from** — even genuine maternal love and precise guilt fail once control becomes *visible* as control (`EVOLUTION.md`). Middle-stage strategy collapses into the same force that doomed Claire's, which is why Anna's later patience is the thing that finally works.

### S7 — Still present, still "protecting" *(ending state)*
- **Step:** N/A.
- **Day/Chapter:** Post-climax.
- **Behavior:** Changed, left behind in the house, still framing everything as protection. Her one post-climax act is a single technically-true text to Brooke the same night, as Anna drives her away ("It isn't safe out there. Come home."), which Brooke never answers. She does not appear again (OQ-06 resolved).
- **Function:** Sarah's love was never fake; that's the tragedy the middle stage is built to prove.

**Sarah's ladder in one line:** loves → restricts → guilts → bargains → shoots. Love and guilt work until they're seen, then collapse into force — the lesson Anna inherits from the *other* direction.

## How the two ladders synchronize with Anna's (the collective's curriculum)

| Whisper stage (`EVOLUTION.md`) | Exemplar | Method | Terminal rung | Lesson passed on |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Early — proximity commands | **Claire** | Force / physical closeness | Breaks the bathroom lock (C6) | Force gets contact, destroys trust |
| Middle — emotional leverage | **Sarah** | Guilt, memory, authority | Fires through the door (S6) | Love/guilt fail once control is visible |
| Later — learned psychology | **Anna** | Study, patience, engineered timing | Brooke gets into Anna's car and initiates the one closeness beat (one-night ending) | Let her choose; win through consent, not force |

The three ladders are one escalating argument the book makes about love-as-possession: each failure narrows the collective's method until Anna arrives at the only version that succeeds — the one that never forces anything. Claire and Sarah must therefore fail *convincingly and sympathetically*, or Anna's success reads as a trick rather than the horrifying endpoint of a learning process.

## Cross-check against locked canon

- Neither Claire nor Sarah states a direct falsehood; Sarah's reassurances are technically true and reframed, Claire is largely nonverbal (`RULES.md`).
- Both retain full memory; both feel pain without protecting against it (Claire's hand, Sarah's disregard mid-siege) (`RULES.md`).
- Personality fragments appear only under the three locked triggers and never resolve into recovery (`RULES.md`, MAIN_CAST): Claire's beach-memory crying (emotional memory), Claire's Cooper restraint (protective threat), Claire's one-second grip release (interruption).
- Methods stay distinct: Claire never uses guilt; Sarah never breaks a barrier to *reach* Brooke — she uses the gun to breach a barrier Brooke put up, which is a different action.
- Synchronization matches `EVOLUTION.md` stage order and hands off via Claire's "Not yet" (C8) and Sarah's collapse (S6).
