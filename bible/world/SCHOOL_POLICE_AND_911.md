# School, Police, and 911

## School progression

School remains open and is where Brooke first realizes the problem extends beyond Claire. The shift is social before it is openly dangerous:

- Former strangers sit pressed together.
- Teachers encourage physical reassurance.
- A student waits outside a bathroom for a friend.
- Hugs last too long.
- Students share foods they previously disliked.
- Staff discourage students from leaving early.
- The school counselor repeats language heard on the news.

Anna enters the Bennett house while Brooke is at school, using the keypad code visible in an old family video.

## Police progression

Police respond normally early in the outbreak. As officers change, their priorities shift from protecting individual autonomy toward keeping families together and preventing separation.

## Brooke's 911 call

Help is technically dispatched, but it is intentionally or unnaturally delayed. The dispatcher begins normally, then moves toward institutionalized reunion language:

- "Try to remain calm."
- "Do not antagonize your mother."
- "She may be experiencing separation distress."
- "Stay inside with her until officers arrive."
- "She is still your mother, Brooke."

Brooke never gave the dispatcher her name. Police arrive too late to help or arrive already changed. The final choice remains a scene-level outline decision.
