# Themes and Motifs

## Central questions

- How do you know when someone who loves you is no longer the same person?
- What happens when love becomes possession?
- Can comfort be more dangerous than an obvious lie?

## Thematic north star

The world does not end because people become obvious monsters. It ends because people stop questioning what feels comforting. Recovered people terrify because they retain and learn to perform humanity.

## Dramatic ironies

- Brooke records life to preserve truth, but her honesty trains Anna to imitate trust.
- Sarah's real love survives as the logic of captivity.
- Anna wins by honoring boundaries more perfectly than Brooke's family can.
- Brooke believes she has been found; the reader learns she has been studied.

## Recurring motifs

- Bright red medicine against white institutional surfaces
- Doors progressing from privacy to lock to barricade to weaponized threshold
- Anna asking permission at thresholds after Claire and Sarah force them
- Cameras, edits, missing frames, and replayed memory
- Green flickers noticed only through attention or footage
- Familiar language repeated until its meaning changes
